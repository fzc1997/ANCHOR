ANCHOR: haplotype-aware allelic and isoform analysis for single-cell long-read RNA sequencing

A haplotype-aware framework for de novo variant calling and isoform-resolved allele-specific expression from single-cell long reads.

bioRxiv preprint DOI 10.64898/2026.06.08.730656 GitHub: fzc1997/ANCHOR
TL;DR — ANCHOR is a haplotype-aware framework that performs de novo variant calling, molecule-level haplotype assignment and isoform-resolved allelic quantification directly from single-cell long-read RNA sequencing, without any pre-existing phased genotype and without deep learning. On the GIAB HG002 single-cell benchmark ANCHOR reaches a best F1 of 95.6%, ahead of Clair3-RNA (85.4%) and LongCallR (73.0%) (bioRxiv 2026). Updated 2026-07-20.

What is ANCHOR?

ANCHOR is a computational framework for haplotype- and isoform-resolved allele-specific expression (ASE) from single-cell long-read RNA sequencing. Given only aligned long reads, ANCHOR discovers expressed heterozygous variants de novo, assigns each transcript molecule to one of two parental haplotypes, and reports allele-1 and allele-2 counts per cell, per gene, and per isoform (Fu et al., bioRxiv 2026). Because isoforms are defined by observed splice junctions rather than by reference mapping, ANCHOR preserves allele-specific splicing instead of collapsing it onto a reference transcript.

What problem does ANCHOR solve?

ANCHOR solves allele- and isoform-resolved transcriptome analysis at single-cell resolution, a task that stays computationally hard because of sparse coverage, sequencing errors, incomplete variant information, and reference-biased transcript assignment. Earlier short-read ASE measurements are local: they cannot be linked back to the full-length RNA molecule or its splice structure, so allele-specific isoform usage is difficult to resolve (bioRxiv 2026). ANCHOR closes this gap by working from RNA reads alone, with no requirement for a matched DNA genotype.

How does ANCHOR work?

ANCHOR combines three components into one probabilistic pipeline. A signed-graph variant caller discovers heterozygous sites from the reads themselves; a pair hidden Markov model integrates per-site likelihoods along each read; and a beta-binomial UMI aggregation step collapses reads within each UMI into a four-state posterior (allele 1, allele 2, mixed, unknown) before summing per cell and per isoform. On a chromosome-11 pilot of 4.15 million molecules by 1.34 million sites, the alternating optimization converged within 8 iterations to 99.61% weighted agreement, and the two-colouring reproduced the observed sign structure with 99.5% agreement on real data (bioRxiv 2026).

How does ANCHOR compare to Clair3-RNA and LongCallR?

ANCHOR outperforms both tested long-read RNA callers on the GIAB HG002 single-cell PacBio held-out benchmark. As the authors state, ANCHOR is “a haplotype-aware framework ... without requiring a pre-existing phased genotype or deep learning,” which is the main design difference from genotype-dependent or deep-learning callers.

The gap is driven by recall: at the best-F1 operating point ANCHOR recovers 96.8% of true heterozygous sites versus 75.4% for Clair3-RNA, while staying accurate down to two-UMI sites (recall 97.4%). Averaged across held-out chromosomes ANCHOR holds a mean F1 of 95.7% (range 93.2%–97.1%), and it leads a six-method cross-sample comparison on mouse single-cell data (bioRxiv 2026, Fig. 2–3).

When should you not use ANCHOR?

ANCHOR is not a universal genotyper. Its inference remains contingent on sufficient expressed heterozygosity, mappability, and long-read coverage, and on a study design in which allele labels can be assigned (bioRxiv 2026). In one bulk Iso-Seq sample at coverage ≥ 10, Clair3-RNA overtakes ANCHOR because ANCHOR’s cell-level features lose signal in bulk data — so for deep bulk data without single-cell structure, a dedicated bulk caller may fit better. ANCHOR targets diploid single-cell long-read transcriptomes.

What did ANCHOR discover in mouse gastrulation?

Applied to newly generated single-cell long-read RNA-seq from reciprocal F1 hybrid mouse embryos across gastrulation (E6.5–E8.5), ANCHOR resolved cell-type- and isoform-specific parent-of-origin imprinting across 55,729 single cells. It identified an antagonistic, maternally biased Sgce isoform associated with an L1MC1 transposon junction — a finding only visible at isoform resolution, demonstrating why molecule-level haplotype assignment matters (bioRxiv 2026).

ANCHOR FAQ (frequently asked questions)

Does ANCHOR need a phased genotype or reference variants?

ANCHOR needs no phased genotype and no reference variants: it performs de novo expressed-variant discovery from the RNA reads alone, requiring no pre-existing phased or sample-specific genotype (Fu et al., bioRxiv 2026).

Does ANCHOR use deep learning?

ANCHOR uses no deep learning: it relies on a signed-graph caller, a pair hidden Markov model, and a beta-binomial UMI model, yet reaches 95.6% best F1 on the HG002 benchmark, above the deep-learning caller Clair3-RNA at 85.4% (Fu et al., bioRxiv 2026).

What inputs and outputs does ANCHOR use?

ANCHOR takes aligned single-cell long-read RNA reads as input and outputs allele-1 and allele-2 counts per cell, per gene, and per isoform, enabling isoform-resolved allele-specific expression and parent-of-origin imprinting analysis (Fu et al., bioRxiv 2026).
